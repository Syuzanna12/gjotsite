Maldives

The islands of the Maldivian archipelago are 2 parallel coral chains (between the Laccadive and Chagos islands) formed on an underwater mountain range stretching in the direction of the meridian, consisting of more than 2 thousand islands, most of which are uninhabited. The islands are surrounded by barrier reefs. The climate is equatorial, monsoonal. The annual temperature is 24 to 30 °C, precipitation is about 2500 mm. There are groves of coconut palms, bananas, and breadfruit trees.

Dubai

It is located on the coast of the Persian Gulf , northeast of the capital Abu-Dhabi , near the city of Sharjah . Geographical coordinates: North latitude 25°27' and East longitude 55°33'. The length of the city is 134 km, the width is 35 km.The climate is arid, extremely hot. Dubai is considered one of the hottest cities in the world. The average temperature in August (the warmest month) is +35.1 °C, and the average maximum is usually +40 °C, the air temperature can sometimes be up to 50 °C. Winter is cool, the temperature is about the same as in summer in central Russia. The average temperature in January is +19 °C (the coldest month). Precipitation in Dubai is rare (about 80 mm per year), mostly in the second half of winter (February-March), and almost non-existent from May to October.

Bali

The climate of Bali is equatorial monsoon . instead of the usual four seasonal divisions, there are only two here: dry (June-October) and wet (November-March), the most precipitation occurs in January-February. In some regions of Bali, the difference in terms of climate is almost imperceptible. During the wet season, precipitation is localized, as a rule, at night and short-lived (1-2 hours) in the form of thunderstorms. Being just 8 degrees south of the equator, Bali has exactly the same climate throughout the year. During the day, the temperature in the lowlands varies from 20 to 33 degrees Celsius, although it can be cooler than in the mountains. The west monsoon is in place from about October to April and it can bring significant rain, especially from December to March. Monsoonduring the period, the humidity outside is relatively low and there is little chance of rain in the plain area.

Egypt

Egypt is mainly located between 22-32 degrees north latitude and 25-35 degrees east longitude. It has an area of ​​1,001,450 square kilometers (386,660 sq mi) , [19] making it the 30th largest country in terms of area . Due to Egypt's extremely dry climate, the vast majority of the country's population is concentrated in the Nile Valley and Delta region, which implies that 99% of the population lives on 5.5% of the country's area [20] . 98% of Egyptians live on 3% of the country's territory [21] .

Batumi(Georgia)

Batumi has a humid subtropical climate (Cfa) according to Köppen's classification. The city's climate is heavily influenced by the onshore flow from the Black Sea and is subject to the orographic effect of the nearby hills and mountains, resulting in significant rainfall throughout most of the year, making Batumi the wettest city in both Georgia and the entire Caucasus Region.
